Summary

Racism is based on incorrect notions that humanity is divided into distinct biological or cultural "races". There is only one "race", however: the human race, beautiful, interesting, diverse and blended.

Even though the supposed biological distinctions don't exist, the practice of classifying (i.e., racialising) people into groups - "races" - persists in order for us to structure our society around these classifications, privileging a certain group (i.e., ourselves: white people) over others. This system of white privilege gives us certain advantages that are not experienced by those we have racialised as minorities.

Racism is the other side of the coin to white privilege: we are privileged at the expense of those we have racialised. Racism isn't only something individual white people are involved with: more importantly, it's in the structure of our society that enables white privilege and the devaluing of the people we have racialised.

Our participation and complicity in racism can be uncomfortable for us white people to hear about, as can be having our privilege challenged. We might feel angry or upset and lash out defensively. But there are better ways to respond and practical steps to take to be actively against the racism that’s in ourselves and our society - which we'll explore next.
